**  6 Semiconductors

- sillicon acts as semiconductor
  - every sillicon atom is bind to his neighbours by 2 electrons
  - at room temperature one of the 2 electrons might leave
  - creating ~holes~ (aka missing electrons) on it
  - the holes give the conductivity

- most semiconductors are doped sillicon
  - added impurities/dopants (eg: phosphorus, boron)
  - resulting in ~N-type~ and ~P-type~ semiconductors
  - depending if electro(N)s or (P)ositive holes carry the current

- =PN Junction= (aka diode)
  [[http://hyperphysics.phy-astr.gsu.edu/hbase/Solids/imgsol/pn2.png]]
   - a P-type+N-type semiconductor
  - leaves the junction with few free charges (aka _bad conductor_)
    - a =depletion region/zone=
    - which can be temporarily removed, with a battery in the right polarity aka *forward bias*

- Types of diodes: zener, photovoltaicell, led

**  9 Building an Audio Amplifier

- 2-stage common-emitter amplifier
  - stage 2 is basically a copy of stage 1
  - in between a capacitor
  - feature: added volume control
    - at the output of the stage 1
    - or at the output of the second stage
  - feature: added an ~emmitter follower~ =2N3904=
    - aka current amplifier
    - aka a common collector configuration
      - because is directly connected to V+ and GND
    - =to not draw current when quiet/0v=
    - feature: added a ~complementary simmetry~ circuit
      - PNP transistor after the NPN of the emitter follower =2N3906=
      - but it's collector to V-, not GND
    - problem: Vout PP gain drop, due transistors internal diode
    - problem: Vout is distorted, due intenal transistor diode breakage voltage (~0.7v)
      - aka ~crossover distortion~
    - solution: 2xdiodes + 2xresistors in series, and in parallel to both transistors, between V+ and V-

** 10 The Ideal Amplifier

- Not everything you want to amplify is audio
  - MRI
  - Sensors: Temperature, Solar Tracker (Pyranometer)

- Ideal
  - high gain (aka "infinite")
  - low output resistance
    - aka 0
    - meaning it can source or sink substantial current
  - high input resistance
    - aka infinite
  - wide freq response (same for all)
  - needs to be good for DC
    - 2-stage common emitter amp IS NOT
    - =due capacitors=

- Circuit ~difference amplifier~
  - Parts
    - NPN transistor
    - resistor collector
    - resistor emitter
    - +V, -V
  - Mirrored
  - Output: voltage between 2 collectors
    - if V1=V2 then Vout=0
    - if V1>V2 then Vout>0
  - Input: on each base
  - To get a DC amplifier, we could GND one input

** 11 Feedback Magic

- Feedback:
  - A change in a system
  - that follows as a result of some earlier change
  - types:
    1) negative: diminishes the orig change, stabilizes
    2) positive: enhances, destabilizing
  - elements:
    - a sensor, quantifies
    - mechanism for acting back

- examples using a normal OPAMP as comparator, with non-electric feedback (slow)
  1) a light follower
     - using 2 LDRs
     - light feedback (resistance)
  2) a temperature controller
     - using a thermister
     - temperature feedback (resistance)
  3) controling a motor direction
     - V+, V-
     - depending on the position of a potentiometer that follows a motor rotation
     - problems: it is unstable
     - potentiometer feedback (resistance)
  4) PWM a light depending on the state of a voltage divider
     - light feedback (resistance)
     - made with a LDR and a resistor
     - V+, V-
